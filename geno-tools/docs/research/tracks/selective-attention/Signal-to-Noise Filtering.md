# Signal-to-Noise Filtering

#attention #selective-attention #L1

The ability to extract relevant information when it is embedded in irrelevant content. In cognitive science, this maps to the cocktail party effect and visual search paradigms.

## Definition
Selective attention filtering involves identifying and processing task-relevant stimuli while suppressing or ignoring task-irrelevant stimuli. The difficulty scales with: (1) the ratio of noise to signal, (2) the similarity between noise and signal, and (3) the presence of competing response-relevant distractors.

## Key Papers
- Shi et al. (2023) — [[GSM-IC (2023)]] showed dramatic drops when irrelevant sentences added to math problems
- Mirzadeh et al. (2024) — [[GSM-Symbolic (2024)]] showed up to 65% drops from single unnecessary clause
- Kaiser et al. (2025) — [[CogniLoad (2025)]] grounded in Cognitive Load Theory, found U-shaped distractor response

## Three Noise Types
1. **Unrelated** — cross-domain filler (easiest to filter)
2. **Related** — same-domain, doesn't answer questions (moderate)
3. **Adversarial** — plausible-but-wrong answers (hardest)

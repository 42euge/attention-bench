# Measuring Progress Toward AGI: A Cognitive Framework (2026)

#paper #literature #framework

**Authors:** Google DeepMind
**Source:** [DeepMind Blog/PDF](https://storage.googleapis.com/deepmind-media/DeepMind.com/Blog/measuring-progress-toward-agi/measuring-progress-toward-agi-a-cognitive-framework.pdf)

## Key Contribution
Introduces a Cognitive Taxonomy deconstructing general intelligence into 10 cognitive faculties: perception, attention, learning, memory, reasoning, metacognition, executive functions, problem-solving, generation, social cognition. Proposes generating "cognitive profiles" per model.

## Problem Addressed
How to measure progress toward AGI beyond single aggregate scores.

## Method
- Literature review of cognitive science
- Framework design with 10 faculties
- Proposal for evaluation protocols

## Key Results
- Attention defined as a first-class cognitive faculty
- Distinct from reasoning (task difficulty vs. attentional load)
- Enables "cognitive profiles" comparing models across dimensions

## Relevance to Our Research
Direct motivation for AttentionBench. Our benchmark isolates attention from reasoning by holding task difficulty constant.

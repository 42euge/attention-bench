# AttentionBench — Research Overview

#attention #L0

Can LLMs focus on what matters and ignore what doesn't? This research explores how to benchmark attention as a cognitive ability in frontier language models, mapping the landscape of existing work, gaps, and novel measurement approaches.

## Sub-Areas

- [[Selective Attention]] — filtering relevant information from noise
- [[Sustained Attention]] — maintaining focus over extended tasks
- [[Divided Attention]] — splitting focus across concurrent demands
- [[Change Detection]] — noticing changes across disruptions

## Evaluation Framework

A good attention benchmark must:
1. **Isolate attention** from reasoning — task difficulty stays constant, only attentional load varies
2. **Show discrimination** — meaningful performance gradient across models (not all 0% or 100%)
3. **Resist contamination** — use fictional content so models can't rely on memorized facts
4. **Map to cognitive science** — grounded in established attention research paradigms

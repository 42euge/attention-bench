# Textual Change Blindness

#attention #change-detection #L1

The failure to detect changes in text when the change coincides with a disruption — a textual analogue of the visual change blindness phenomenon discovered by Rensink, O'Regan & Clark (1997).

## In Human Cognition
Change blindness occurs when observers fail to notice large changes in visual scenes during brief disruptions (blinks, saccades, flickers, cuts). The critical insight: attention is required to detect change, and disruptions reset attentional focus.

## LLM Analogue
Present passage A → disruptor paragraph(s) → passage A' with one factual change. Ask: "What changed?" The disruption serves as the textual equivalent of a visual flicker. Hypothesis: models will show reduced detection rates with longer disruptors, especially for minor changes.

## Unadapted Paradigms
- **Flicker paradigm** (alternating A/blank/A') — adapted in our benchmark
- **Mudsplash** (change + simultaneous salient distraction) — untested
- **Gradual change** (progressive alteration over document) — untested
- **Inattentional blindness** (missing unexpected events while focused) — untested
